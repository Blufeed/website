The idea we got from Mark Cuban

Mark Cuban recently posted something on Bluesky that resonated with us and sparked an exciting idea for Blufeed. Here’s what he shared:

“Congrats to @bluesky for all the new users. My 2 cents. Find every news source that has an RSS feed or equivalent and create accounts that show their public deeds on here under @johndoetimesfeed. That way we all get access to news without having to wait for them to create accounts first.”

We couldn’t agree more with Mark Cuban’s vision. As Bluesky continues to grow, it’s clear that one of the biggest opportunities for innovation lies in bridging the gap between external content and the vibrant, decentralized community emerging on this platform. Cuban’s idea to aggregate news from various sources into Bluesky via RSS feeds immediately sparked an idea for us at Blufeed: what if we could automate this process and bring external content into Bluesky seamlessly?

This led to the birth of Blufeed, an RSS aggregator designed specifically for Bluesky. Blufeed takes Cuban’s concept to the next level by enabling users to pull in news from any RSS feed (or similar sources) and share it automatically on Bluesky. We built Blufeed with the intent to empower the community, ensuring that everyone has access to fresh, up-to-date news without waiting for sources to create official accounts themselves.

By integrating external content directly into Bluesky, Blufeed helps shape a more interconnected ecosystem, enabling users to share knowledge and information in real-time, while preserving the core values of decentralization. With Blufeed, you get a solution that turns the vision of quick and direct access to news into a reality.

We believe this kind of innovation will fuel the future of social media, especially as platforms like Bluesky continue to emphasize decentralized control and open-source collaboration. Blufeed is working with other cool apps and developers to enhance the ecosystem and provide tools that make Bluesky an even more vibrant place to share and discover content.

Bluesky’s openness, combined with tools like Blufeed, unlocks the potential for a truly decentralized, community-powered network. It’s a place where news, opinions, and insights can flow freely and instantly, without barriers. Together, we can continue building the future of social media—faster, more collaborative, and always with user-first innovation.

We want to thank Mark Cuban for his inspiring idea and remind everyone that at Blufeed, we’re just getting started. Let’s keep innovating together!

The resistance is coming to Bluesky

Bluesky is not just another social platform; it’s a revolution in the making. Built on the principles of openness, decentralization, and community-driven innovation, it offers fertile ground for creators, developers, and visionaries to reshape how we connect and share online. With the Resistance—a wave of innovators, creators, open-source advocates, and tech enthusiasts—joining Bluesky, this platform stands poised to disrupt the social media landscape as we know it.

Bluesky is a decentralized social network, leveraging the AT Protocol to create an ecosystem where users and developers are free to innovate without the constraints of traditional walled gardens. This opens a world of possibilities. Users can develop custom algorithms to choose how they engage with content, reflecting their personal interests and values. The platform’s API and developer-friendly infrastructure make it easy to build tools and apps that integrate seamlessly with Bluesky. Whether you’re a creator looking to build communities or a developer experimenting with new features, Bluesky offers unparalleled flexibility. The stage is set for groundbreaking projects—think decentralized content curation, niche community hubs, and AI-driven insights tailored to individual needs.

At the heart of Bluesky lies a commitment to open-source principles. The platform’s code is a shared resource, encouraging developers to contribute, iterate, and innovate together. This fosters a culture of transparency and collaboration that’s rare in today’s tech world. The Resistance embodies these ideals: community-driven development allows developers to work together to enhance Bluesky’s infrastructure, fix bugs, and introduce features that benefit everyone. Shared knowledge through open-source tools and libraries lowers barriers to entry, enabling even small teams to make a big impact. Creative synergy flourishes, as ideas are refined and improved through collective effort.

Bluesky’s decentralized approach is more than a technical achievement; it’s a philosophical shift. It challenges the centralized control of traditional social media, empowering users to take ownership of their data, identities, and online experiences. This decentralization creates opportunities for data sovereignty, allowing users to own their content and choose how it’s shared or monetized. It ensures resilient communities, where no single entity can dictate the rules or silence voices. Decentralization also enables scalable innovation, as independent developers can create interoperable apps, enriching the ecosystem without needing approval from a central authority.

The Resistance isn’t a group of rebels with lasers (though that sounds fun); it’s a movement of creators, developers, and dreamers who see the potential of Bluesky to change how we interact online. Whether you’re building the next killer app, sharing knowledge through open-source projects, or fostering inclusive communities, Bluesky is the canvas for your vision. Decentralized, open, and full of possibilities, Bluesky is the platform where your ideas can thrive. The Resistance is coming—will you join us in shaping the future of social media?

Blufeed is a proud part of the Resistance, actively collaborating with exciting new apps to expand and enhance the Bluesky ecosystem. As an RSS aggregator designed to seamlessly connect with Bluesky, Blufeed enables automatic posting and content sharing, bringing valuable external insights into the platform. By working with innovative tools and creators, Blufeed is helping to shape a more interconnected and dynamic social experience on Bluesky. This collaboration exemplifies the spirit of open-source and decentralized development, where different players unite to build a more inclusive and feature-rich environment for users. Together, we’re creating a more vibrant ecosystem where creativity and collaboration thrive.

Let’s build something extraordinary together.

Farewell to X, and welcome to Bluesky

For many years, Twitter was a cornerstone of my digital life. It started simply, as a way to stay connected with family and friends. In its early days, it was a refreshing space where people could share thoughts, updates, and humor without the weight of constant debate or divisiveness. It felt like a small, personal town square where everyone’s voice mattered.

As time went on, my interests evolved, and so did my Twitter experience. I found myself deeply immersed in the vibrant Crypto Twitter community. It was exhilarating—an endless stream of innovation, ideas, and collaboration. People shared insights about blockchain technology, decentralized finance, and the vision of a better, freer internet. For a while, it was magical.

Then, when Elon Musk entered the picture, I was filled with renewed hope. His innovations, including the development of Grok, promised to reshape the platform and bring fresh perspectives to AI, technology, and the human experience. Elon’s vision seemed like the kind of revolution Twitter / X needed.

Unfortunately, the excitement didn’t last. Over time, the new X platform became inundated with misinformation and political rhetoric. Conversations turned into battlegrounds, and it felt like the original purpose of Twitter—to connect, share, and inspire—was lost. The noise of constant outrage drowned out meaningful dialogue, and the sense of community that drew me in was nowhere to be found.

I tried to adapt, muting and blocking accounts to curate my feed. But it became clear that the platform itself had changed fundamentally. Instead of being a place of connection and discovery, X had become a divisive, polarizing space that left me more drained than inspired.

So, like many others, I decided to leave. It wasn’t an easy choice, but it felt necessary for my mental clarity and peace. Platforms like Bluesky, which capture the spirit of what Twitter once was, have drawn my attention, and I’m exploring it with optimism and excitement.

Leaving X doesn’t mean I’m giving up on the idea of meaningful digital connections. It means I’m seeking them elsewhere, in places where dialogue thrives over division, and innovation takes center stage without the baggage of negativity.

For those who still find value in X, I wish you well. For me, it was time to move on.
